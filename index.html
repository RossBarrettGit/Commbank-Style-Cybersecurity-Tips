Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Use Encrypted Websites and Watch What You Click

Don't click on any links by going straight to the source (i.e. your financial firms website).
Stick to one webpage at a time, make sure the websites and encrypted and their URL starts with https, and log out when you're done.

Use Strong Passwords and Protect Them!

Don't share passwords, use a unique one for each account and change them regularly.
Use a password manager.
Make your password at least 10 characters long and make it contain unpredictable letters, numbers and special characters.

Use Your Device, Maintain Its Security, and Make Sure It Updates Automatically

Use an antivirus, anti-spam and spyware detection. Keep your security software up to date automatically.
Use a multi-factor authenticator (MFA) and verify that it uses data encryption
Get a full understanding of your MFA's terms of service and costs before you sign an agreement.

Use Secure Wifi Networks

Use encrypted networks to access your financial accounts.
Ensure that your home wifi network uses the strongest encryption and a strong password.

Review All Correspondence From Your Financial Institutions

Review your account activity and monthly account statements thoroughly as soon as they are available.
Make sure your financial institution has your current contact information and that you regularly receive statements.
If you see a mistake or unauthorized activity in your account, contact your financial institution immediately.

Reduce Your Sharing of Your Personal Information As Much As Possible

This Includes your Social Security number, credit card number and bank and utility account numbers.

Research Companies Before Accepting an Offer or Advert

Type the company name into your favorite search engine.

Back Up Your Files

Copy important files into a removable disc or external hard drive and store in a safe place.
